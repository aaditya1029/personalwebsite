About

A little about me

I'm a third-year Geomatics Engineering student at Kathmandu University, and honestly, maps have a way of pulling me in that nothing else does. There's something satisfying about taking raw coordinates and turning them into a picture that actually tells you something — where the river bends, where the land rises, where a road should go.

My interest in GIS, digital mapping, spatial analysis, and land surveying started in the classroom but it didn't stay there. I spend a lot of my free time inside ArcGIS Pro and QGIS just experimenting — digitizing old maps, building layers, trying to make sense of datasets until they click.

I'm still early in this journey, and I'm fine saying that. I'm not claiming to be an expert — I'm someone who shows up consistently, keeps learning the tools of the trade, and is genuinely excited about where the geospatial industry is heading. That's the version of myself I'm trying to put on this page.

When I'm not buried in a map, I'm usually thinking about the next thing I want to learn — better surveying technique, a new ArcGIS workflow, or just understanding a place a little more deeply than before.

— Aaditya
